# MineAid HMS — Strategic narrative for UMA (Underground Mining Alliance) at Obuasi

**Purpose of this document**  
To structure a focused presentation to **UMA Obuasi**—the **largest underground mining contractor** on the **AngloGold Ashanti Obuasi** site—showing how **MineAid HMS** **digitizes workflows UMA’s First Aiders already run**, and removes the **paper-to-Excel copying** burden at the **First Aid Post (FAP)**. This is a **source document** (talking points and logic), not the only possible slide order.

**In-app deck (super admin):** **Pitch: UMA Obuasi** → `/super-admin/pitch-uma-obuasi`

**Related (operator / AGA-facing narrative):** `docs/AGA_OBUASI_MINEAID_PITCH_SOURCE.md` — in-app **Pitch: AGA Obuasi mine** → `/super-admin/pitch-aga-obuasi`.

**Audience (intended)**  
UMA **management and health/OHSE leads** who fund tools and set policy—but frame the story so **First Aiders** (primary **end users**, alongside **AGAHF emergency nurses**) see themselves in it. **Buyers** and **daily users** are not the same; success requires **both**: leadership buys **governance and efficiency**; First Aiders adopt because the system **matches how they already work**.

## 3. The pain you can describe without blaming anyone

### 3.1 Paper at FAP → Excel at UMA (double entry)

**Observation**  
First Aiders **travel to the FAP** to **copy** what’s on **paper** about **UMA staff** into **UMA’s Excel**—a second system of record built by **re-keying**.

**Effects**  
- **Time** lost for First Aiders and FAP staff.  
- **Errors** (transcription, incomplete fields).  
- **No single governed digital thread** that both parties trust the same way.  
- **Lag**: leadership and M&E see **spreadsheet reality**, not **live** operational truth.

### 3.2 Processes MineAid already mirrors (credibility line)

Several product areas were **informed by real routines** on site—including **hydration testing**, **employee follow-ups**, and **Work fitness & medications**—the kinds of tasks **First Aiders handle daily**.

**Message**  
MineAid is not asking UMA to invent **new bureaucracy**. It is offering to **digitize and structure** what **already happens**—with **roles**, **audit trails**, and **one place** to see **their workforce health picture** (within agreed **access rules**).

### 3.3 End users vs buyers

**End users**  
**UMA First Aiders** (and **AGAHF nurses**) live in the **workflow**. If the tool **adds friction**, adoption fails.

**Buyers**  
UMA **management** needs **ROI**, **risk reduction**, **alignment with AGA/AGAHF**, and a **clear data and contracting story**.

**Bridge**  
Pitch **efficiency and accuracy for First Aiders** in the same breath as **governance for the company**.

---

## 4. National direction and defensibility (short)

Ghana and serious health systems expect **digitized**, **traceable** health information. **Parallel paper + Excel** on a large regulated site is **hard to defend** when questions arise about **continuity**, **privacy**, and **oversight**. MineAid aligns **contractor workforce health** with that direction **without** pretending one spreadsheet is a system of record.

---

## 5. What “good” looks like for UMA (outcomes)

- **First Aiders** work from **authorized digital access** to **structured data** (within **tenant + RBAC**), not **copying binders**.  
- **Hydration/testing, follow-ups, meds declaration** live in **modules** that match **existing practice**, not alien forms.  
- **UMA leadership** gets **dashboards and reports** derived from **one disciplined pipeline**, not **late Excel consolidation**.  
- **AGAHF** retains **clinical authority** where appropriate; **boundaries** are explicit in **roles and agreements**—not ambiguous “who owns this row in Excel.”

---

## 6. MineAid response (tailored bullets)

- **Mining-native HMS** — not a generic hospital EMR bolt-on.  
- **Structured records** — visits, programmes, testing, incidents—with **auditability**.  
- **Contractor-appropriate model** — **tenant** and **role** design so **UMA** can see **UMA workforce** data **they are entitled to**, without breaking **clinical governance**.  
- **Modules aligned to First Aider reality** — e.g. **testing/hydration**, **Employee wellbeing**-style **follow-ups** and **medication declaration** patterns.  
- **Less duplicate entry** — goal is to **feed** operational need from **governed capture** at the point of care, not **re-keying from paper**.

---

## 7. Partnership framing (respectful)

- MineAid **supports** UMA’s **duty of care** to its workforce; it does **not** replace **AGA** or **AGAHF** governance.  
- **Name contracting reality**: **UMA**, **AGAHF**, **operator**—**data sharing** and **procurement** must be **explicit**.  
- **Phased path**: confirm priorities with **First Aiders** and **UMA OHSE/management** → **bounded pilot** (e.g. one workforce slice + agreed modules) → measure **time saved**, **error reduction**, **reporting quality** → scale.

---

## 8. Suggested next steps

1. **Discovery** — Short session with **First Aiders** and **UMA leads**: map today’s **FAP → Excel** steps and **daily modules** (hydration, follow-ups, meds).  
2. **Pilot scope** — Agree **which population** and **which MineAid modules** first; align **AGAHF** on **handover** from **paper copy** to **digital workflow**.  
3. **Governance** — Data roles, retention, who can see what under **UMA tenant** vs **site clinical** record.  
4. **Demo** — Live walkthrough: **First Aider day** + **management report**—not a generic product tour.
