## 1. Definitions

Terms used but not defined in this BAA have the meanings given in HIPAA and its implementing regulations (45 C.F.R. §§ 160.103, 164.103, 164.501, and related provisions). For purposes of this BAA:

- **Protected Health Information** or **PHI** means individually identifiable health information, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103, that Covered Entity creates, receives, maintains, or transmits in connection with the Services and that Business Associate receives from or on behalf of Covered Entity, or creates, receives, maintains, or transmits on behalf of Covered Entity.
- **Services** means the MineAid HMS healthcare management system and related hosting, support, and other services provided by Business Associate to Covered Entity under the Commercial Agreement or other written agreement between the Parties, which involve the creation, receipt, maintenance, or transmission of PHI.
- **Commercial Agreement** means the subscription or commercial agreement between the Parties for the Services, as amended from time to time.
- **Security Incident** means the attempted or successful unauthorised access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system, as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 164.304.
- **Breach** has the meaning given in 45 C.F.R. § 164.402 (unauthorised acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of unsecured PHI that compromises the security or privacy of such information, with certain exceptions).

## 2. Permitted uses and disclosures of PHI

2.1 **Performance of Services.** Business Associate may use and disclose PHI only as necessary to perform the Services for or on behalf of Covered Entity, or as required by law, and in accordance with this BAA. Business Associate will not use or disclose PHI for any other purpose, including for Business Associate’s own marketing or for the benefit of any third party, except as permitted by this BAA or as required by law.

2.2 **Minimum necessary.** To the extent practicable, Business Associate will use, disclose, and request only the minimum PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose of the use, disclosure, or request, in accordance with the minimum necessary standard under the Privacy Rule.

2.3 **Safeguards.** Business Associate will use appropriate safeguards to prevent use or disclosure of PHI other than as provided by this BAA, and will implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of electronic PHI that Business Associate creates, receives, maintains, or transmits on behalf of Covered Entity, in accordance with the Security Rule (45 C.F.R. §§ 164.308, 164.310, 164.312) and HITECH.

2.4 **Reporting.** Business Associate will report to Covered Entity any use or disclosure of PHI not permitted by this BAA of which Business Associate becomes aware, and any Security Incident of which Business Associate becomes aware. With respect to a Breach of unsecured PHI, Business Associate will notify Covered Entity without unreasonable delay and in no event later than **60 days** after discovery of the Breach, in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.410. The notice will include, to the extent possible: (a) the identification of each individual whose unsecured PHI has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed during the Breach; (b) a brief description of what happened; (c) the types of unsecured PHI involved; and (d) steps Business Associate is taking to investigate, mitigate, and prevent further Breaches. Business Associate will provide such additional information as Covered Entity may reasonably request and as is available to Business Associate.

2.5 **Subcontractors and agents.** Business Associate will ensure that any subcontractor or agent that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of Business Associate agrees in writing to the same restrictions, conditions, and requirements that apply to Business Associate under this BAA with respect to such PHI (including the implementation of reasonable and appropriate safeguards for electronic PHI). Business Associate remains responsible for compliance with this BAA and for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors and agents to the same extent as if such acts or omissions were performed by Business Associate.

2.6 **Access to records.** To the extent that Business Associate maintains PHI in a Designated Record Set (as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 164.501) or in a manner that permits identification of individuals, Business Associate will, within a reasonable time (and in any event within **15 business days** of a written request from Covered Entity), make such PHI available to Covered Entity so that Covered Entity may meet its obligations under 45 C.F.R. § 164.524 (access by individuals). If an individual requests access to PHI directly from Business Associate, Business Associate will forward the request to Covered Entity and will cooperate with Covered Entity in fulfilling the request.

2.7 **Amendment of PHI.** To the extent Business Associate maintains PHI in a Designated Record Set, Business Associate will, within a reasonable time (and in any event within **15 business days** of a written request from Covered Entity), make amendments to PHI as directed by Covered Entity so that Covered Entity may meet its obligations under 45 C.F.R. § 164.526 (amendments). If an individual requests an amendment directly from Business Associate, Business Associate will forward the request to Covered Entity and will cooperate with Covered Entity in fulfilling the request.

2.8 **Accounting of disclosures.** Business Associate will, within a reasonable time (and in any event within **30 days** of a written request from Covered Entity), provide to Covered Entity or to an individual (as directed by Covered Entity) information collected in accordance with this BAA to permit Covered Entity to respond to a request for an accounting of disclosures under 45 C.F.R. § 164.528. Business Associate will maintain a record of disclosures of PHI (other than for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations; for the individual; pursuant to an authorisation; or as otherwise excepted under the Privacy Rule) and of Security Incidents, as necessary to provide such accounting. If an individual requests an accounting directly from Business Associate, Business Associate will forward the request to Covered Entity and will cooperate with Covered Entity in fulfilling the request.

2.9 **Government access.** If a government agency or law enforcement official requests PHI from Business Associate, Business Associate will, unless legally prohibited, notify Covered Entity of the request in writing before disclosing the PHI (or as soon thereafter as permitted), so that Covered Entity may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

## 3. Obligations of covered entity

Covered Entity will: (a) notify Business Associate of any limitation(s) in its notice of privacy practices to the extent that such limitation may affect Business Associate’s use or disclosure of PHI; (b) notify Business Associate of any changes in, or revocation of, permission by an individual to use or disclose PHI, to the extent that such changes may affect Business Associate’s use or disclosure of PHI; and (c) notify Business Associate of any restriction to the use or disclosure of PHI that Covered Entity has agreed to under 45 C.F.R. § 164.522, to the extent that such restriction may affect Business Associate’s use or disclosure of PHI. Business Associate will comply with such limitations, changes, revocations, and restrictions to the extent they are consistent with this BAA and applicable law.

## 4. Term and termination

4.1 **Term.** This BAA is effective as of the Effective Date and will continue for so long as Business Associate creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of Covered Entity under the Commercial Agreement or otherwise, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this section.

4.2 **Termination for cause.** Either Party may terminate this BAA and the Commercial Agreement (if the breach also constitutes a material breach thereof) if the other Party materially breaches this BAA and fails to cure the breach within **30 days** of receiving written notice specifying the breach. Covered Entity may terminate this BAA immediately if Covered Entity reasonably determines that Business Associate has violated a material term of this BAA and cure is not possible.

4.3 **Termination by Covered Entity.** Covered Entity may terminate this BAA for convenience upon **90 days** prior written notice to Business Associate. In that case, the Commercial Agreement may also be terminated in accordance with its terms.

4.4 **Effect of termination.** Upon termination or expiration of this BAA (or of the Commercial Agreement, if it involves PHI), Business Associate will, at Covered Entity’s election, either: (a) return to Covered Entity all PHI that Business Associate still maintains in any form, or (b) destroy such PHI and provide Covered Entity with written certification of destruction, in each case to the extent feasible and except where Business Associate is required to retain copies by law. If return or destruction is not feasible, Business Associate will extend the protections of this BAA to such PHI and limit further use and disclosure to those purposes that make return or destruction infeasible. This section survives termination of this BAA.

## 5. No third-party beneficiary

Nothing in this BAA is intended to create any third-party beneficiary rights, including in favour of any individual who is a subject of PHI. The Parties’ obligations are solely to each other.

## 6. Interpretation and compliance with HIPAA

6.1 **Interpretation.** This BAA will be interpreted in a manner consistent with HIPAA and its implementing regulations. To the extent any provision of this BAA is inconsistent with HIPAA or its implementing regulations, or with any amendment thereto, the Parties will amend this BAA in good faith to the extent necessary to comply. In the interim, HIPAA and its implementing regulations will control.

6.2 **Compliance.** Each Party will comply with applicable HIPAA requirements with respect to the PHI that it creates, receives, maintains, or transmits. Business Associate will make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services for purposes of determining Covered Entity’s compliance with the Privacy Rule, to the extent required by 45 C.F.R. § 164.504(e)(2)(ii)(G).

## 7. General

7.1 **Relationship to Commercial Agreement.** This BAA supplements and is incorporated into the Commercial Agreement (or other written agreement under which the Services are provided). In the event of a conflict between this BAA and the Commercial Agreement with respect to the handling of PHI, this BAA will control. All other terms of the Commercial Agreement remain in effect.

7.2 **Governing law.** This BAA is governed by the laws of the **United States** and the state or jurisdiction in which Covered Entity is located, to the extent required to give effect to HIPAA and its implementing regulations. To the extent not inconsistent with HIPAA, the laws of **Ghana** may apply to the general contract terms between the Parties (e.g. notice, assignment).

7.3 **Notices.** Notices under this BAA will be in writing and sent to the contact details set out above (or as updated in writing). Notices to Business Associate may be sent to [nuestman@icloud.com](mailto:nuestman@icloud.com). Notices related to Breaches or Security Incidents should be clearly marked as such.

7.4 **Amendment.** This BAA may be amended only in writing signed by both Parties. Either Party may propose an amendment to align with changes in HIPAA or its implementing regulations; the Parties will negotiate in good faith to adopt such amendments.
