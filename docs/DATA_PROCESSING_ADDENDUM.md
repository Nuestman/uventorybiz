# MineAid HMS — Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

**Last updated:** April 2026  

This Data Processing Addendum (“**DPA**”) forms part of the agreement between:

**Processor:** **Nuestman Tech Solutions** (trading as applicable; registered with the Office of the Registrar of Companies (ORC), Ghana, and the Data Protection Commission (DPC) as **Nuestman Links Enterprise**)  
Email: [nuestman@icloud.com](mailto:nuestman@icloud.com)

**Controller:** **[Customer legal name]** (“**Controller**”)

This DPA applies where Processor processes personal data (including special-category / health-related data where applicable) on behalf of Controller when providing **MineAid HMS** under the **Commercial Agreement** and **Terms of Service**.

---

## 1. Subject matter, nature, and purpose

1.1 **Subject matter.** Processing of **Customer Data** (personal data submitted by Controller or its authorised users to the Service).

1.2 **Nature.** Hosting, storage, retrieval, backup, support, security monitoring, and service improvement that does not conflict with section 4.

1.3 **Purpose.** Providing the MineAid HMS service as described in the Commercial Agreement and documentation.

1.4 **Duration.** For the Subscription Term and as needed to export or delete data in accordance with the Privacy Policy and Commercial Agreement.

---

## 2. Controller instructions

2.1 Processor will process Customer Data only on **documented instructions** from Controller, including via configuration of the Service and use by Authorised Users, unless required by law (in which case Processor will inform Controller unless prohibited).

2.2 Controller is responsible for the **lawfulness** of processing instructions and for **notices** to data subjects.

---

## 3. Confidentiality

Processor ensures that persons authorised to process Customer Data are bound by **confidentiality** obligations or are under a statutory duty of confidentiality.

---

## 4. Security

4.1 Processor will implement **appropriate technical and organisational measures** having regard to the state of the art, cost, and risks—including encryption in transit where standard for the Service, access controls, and logging as described in the **Security** page and Privacy Policy.

4.2 Controller acknowledges that **no system is perfectly secure** and will use strong passwords, role design, and operational discipline on its side.

---

## 5. Sub-processors

5.1 Controller **authorises** Processor to engage **sub-processors** listed or updated in **Subprocessors & infrastructure** (`/legal/subprocessors` or the then-current published list). Processor will give notice of material changes where required by contract or law.

5.2 Where required by applicable law, Processor will impose **data protection terms** on sub-processors that are substantially similar to this DPA.

---

## 6. Data subject rights

6.1 Taking into account the nature of processing, Processor will **assist** Controller by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as possible, for fulfilment of data subject requests (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection) under applicable law.

6.2 Requests received directly by Processor will be **forwarded** to Controller unless Processor is legally required to act.

---

## 7. Breach notification

Processor will notify Controller **without undue delay** after becoming aware of a **personal data breach** affecting Customer Data, with information available at that time, and will cooperate with Controller’s obligations regarding notification to authorities or data subjects where applicable.

---

## 8. Return and deletion

On termination or expiry, Processor will, at Controller’s choice where technically feasible, **delete or return** Customer Data in line with the Commercial Agreement and Privacy Policy, subject to legal retention requirements.

---

## 9. Audits and records

9.1 Processor will make available information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA.

9.2 **On-site audits** (if any) will be conducted during business hours, with reasonable notice, and limited to what is required by applicable law or regulation, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

---

## 10. International transfers

Where Customer Data is transferred outside the country of establishment of Controller, Processor will implement **appropriate safeguards** required by applicable law (e.g. adequacy decisions, standard contractual clauses, or other lawful mechanisms). Details may be set out in the Subprocessors list or Order.

---

## 11. Ghana Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843)

Where Act 843 applies, the Parties intend this DPA to satisfy **written contract** requirements between controller and processor under the Act, in addition to any registration or notification duties of each Party.

---

## 12. Order of precedence

If this DPA conflicts with the **Commercial Agreement** on **data protection** matters, the **stricter or more specific** provision for the protection of personal data prevails. The Commercial Agreement prevails for **fees, liability caps, and term**.
