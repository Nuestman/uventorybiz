## 2. Personal data we collect and why

### 2.1 Account and administrative data

- **Name, email address, role, and similar identifiers** for user accounts and tenant administration.  
- **Legal basis (GDPR):** Contract (performance of the service), legitimate interest (security and administration).  
- **Ghana Act 843:** Processed in accordance with the data protection principles (lawfulness, fairness, purpose limitation, etc.).

### 2.2 Healthcare and operational data (including PHI)

When the Service is used for mining healthcare operations, we may process:

- **Patient and clinical data** (e.g. identities, medical visits, vitals, incidents, drug/alcohol testing, work restrictions).  
- **Data about personnel** (e.g. assignments, duties, locations) where you input such data into the Service.

This may include **Protected Health Information (PHI)** as defined under HIPAA when the Service is used by or on behalf of a HIPAA-covered entity. Where we act as a business associate, we do so under a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) and in line with HIPAA’s Privacy and Security Rules.

- **Legal basis (GDPR):** Contract, legal obligation (e.g. health and safety), and/or consent where required.  
- **Ghana Act 843:** Processed for specified, legitimate purposes with appropriate safeguards; sensitive data (including health data) handled in line with the Act.

### 2.3 Technical and usage data

- **Logs, IP address, device/browser data, and usage information** necessary for security, availability, and support.  
- **Legal basis (GDPR):** Legitimate interest (security, fraud prevention, improving the service).  
- **Ghana Act 843:** Processed in line with the principles of necessity and purpose limitation.

We do **not** sell your personal data.

Where we process PHI, we do so only as permitted by our BAA and HIPAA.

## 4. Legal basis for processing (GDPR)

Where GDPR applies:

- **Contract:** Processing necessary to provide the Service and perform our agreement with you or your organisation.  
- **Legal obligation:** Processing necessary to comply with laws (e.g. health and safety, data protection).  
- **Legitimate interest:** Processing for our or a third party’s legitimate interests (e.g. security, support, improvement of the Service), where not overridden by your rights.  
- **Consent:** Where we rely on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

## 5. Data retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes set out in this policy, including:

- **Account and administrative data:** For the duration of the contract and a reasonable period thereafter (e.g. for legal and audit purposes).  
- **Healthcare/operational data (including PHI):** As required by contract, applicable law, or professional obligations (e.g. medical record retention).  
- **Logs and security-related data:** As needed for security and compliance, in line with our retention schedule.

After the retention period, we delete or anonymise data so it no longer identifies you, except where we must keep it for legal or regulatory reasons.

## 6. Your rights

### 6.1 Rights under GDPR (EEA/UK)

Where GDPR applies, you have the right to:

- **Access:** Obtain confirmation as to whether we process your data and a copy of your data.  
- **Rectification:** Have inaccurate personal data corrected.  
- **Erasure (“right to be forgotten”):** Request deletion in the circumstances set out in the GDPR.  
- **Restriction:** Request restriction of processing in certain situations.  
- **Data portability:** Receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format where the legal basis is contract or consent.  
- **Object:** Object to processing based on legitimate interest (including profiling).  
- **Withdraw consent:** Where we rely on consent, withdraw it at any time.  
- **Lodge a complaint** with a supervisory authority (e.g. in your country of residence or in Ghana if applicable).

To exercise these rights, contact us using the details in section 1.

### 6.2 Rights under Ghana’s Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843)

Under Act 843, data subjects have rights including access, correction, and deletion in accordance with the Act. We will respond to requests in line with the Act and any guidelines issued by the Data Protection Commission. Contact us using the details in section 1.

### 6.3 Rights under HIPAA (US)

Where HIPAA applies, individuals have rights as set out in the HIPAA Privacy Rule (e.g. access, amendment, accounting of disclosures, restrictions, confidential communications). If we process your PHI as a business associate, those rights are exercised through the covered entity (e.g. your employer or the healthcare provider). We support the covered entity in fulfilling such requests as required by our BAA.

## 7. Security and confidentiality

We implement technical and organisational measures to protect personal data (including PHI where applicable), including:

- **Access control:** Role-based access, strong authentication, and tenant isolation.  
- **Encryption:** Data encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest where appropriate.  
- **Integrity and availability:** Monitoring, backups, and procedures to protect against unauthorised access, loss, or alteration.  
- **Policies and training:** Internal policies and training on data protection and, where relevant, HIPAA.

These measures are aligned with GDPR (Article 32), HIPAA Security Rule requirements where we act as a business associate, and Ghana’s Data Protection Act 843.

## 8. Data breaches

- **GDPR:** We will notify the relevant supervisory authority and affected data subjects where a breach is likely to result in a risk to rights and freedoms, in line with GDPR requirements.  
- **Ghana Act 843:** We will report serious breaches to the Data Protection Commission as required by the Act and any applicable guidelines.  
- **HIPAA:** Where we are a business associate and a breach affects PHI, we will notify the covered entity in accordance with our BAA and HIPAA breach notification rules.
